# Proposal Generator — Forms & Checklists

Intake, the client-centric proposal template, and the pre-send quality checklist. Firm
specifics are decided per organization (see [`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md)); this file never
hardcodes them. Every price and outcome metric is **HUMAN INPUT**.

---

## Proposal Intake (the firm-specific questionnaire for adapting this skill)

Ask these once if the firm's details aren't yet decided. Ask as one numbered list and
**WAIT** for answers; then record them wherever your team keeps these decisions. Ask only
what's missing.

1. **Firm name & wordmark** — exact name used in "Prepared by", including punctuation?
2. **One-line descriptor** — how the firm describes what it is?
3. **Promise** — the firm's core value statement / what every engagement promises?
4. **Key messages** — 5-9 points that should land in any proposal (e.g. "one partner for
   the whole journey", "partner not vendor")?
5. **Differentiators** — what makes this firm different from competitors?
6. **Business model** — one line (e.g. phased projects + retainer)?
7. **Service lines** — for each: a name, a one-line summary, and the **client pain it
   solves** (in client language, NO internal code)?
8. **Who prepares / signs** — title and contact line to put in the signature block?

---

## Discovery Recap (ask these per proposal if discovery facts are missing)

These are **client-specific**, not firm-specific — gathered fresh for each proposal.

1. **Client name** and the project/engagement title?
2. **Their pain in their own words** — a quote you can repeat back?
3. **Current-state cost** — their numbers (hours/week, error cost, missed revenue)?
4. **What "success" looks like** to them?
5. **Constraints** — timeline, budget signal, decision-makers, existing systems?
6. **Pricing** — confirmed figures for each phase and the retainer (**HUMAN INPUT** —
   never invent)?

## Quality Checklist

Run before sending any proposal.

### Client-centricity
- [ ] Opens with THEIR pain, not the firm's services?
- [ ] Uses THEIR words from discovery (quoted back)?
- [ ] Outcomes emphasized over deliverables?
- [ ] No internal service codes visible?
- [ ] Phased journey is clear (journey, not service menu)?

### Partnership positioning
- [ ] Long-term relationship implied?
- [ ] Retainer option included?
- [ ] Not positioned as a one-time vendor?
- [ ] Firm's differentiators / key messages reflected?

### Completeness
- [ ] ASSESS phase included?
- [ ] ROI projection included (computed from the client's numbers)?
- [ ] Investment clearly stated with `$[HUMAN INPUT]` placeholders — no invented prices?
- [ ] Every outcome metric is a bracketed placeholder, not a carried-over figure?
- [ ] Next steps are specific?
- [ ] Valid-until date included?

### Firm fidelity
- [ ] Firm name / wordmark match the firm's decided details exactly?
- [ ] Promise and messaging match the firm's positioning (not improvised)?

---

## Success Criteria

- Client feels understood — the proposal reflects THEIR specific situation.
- The journey is clear — phases, not a service menu.
- Investment is framed as value, not cost.
- Partnership is implied, not just a project.
- No internal service codes or technical jargon visible.
- The client can share the proposal with stakeholders without confusion.
- Next steps are specific and easy.
- No price and no outcome metric was invented — all are HUMAN INPUT or computed from the
  client's own numbers.
