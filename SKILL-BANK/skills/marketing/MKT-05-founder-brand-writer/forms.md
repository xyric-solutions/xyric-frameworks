# Founder Brand Writer — Forms & Checklists

Loaded on demand for content-generation forms, quality checklists, and collaboration
templates. Founder names, angles, and voices are the organization's founder decisions; this
file never hardcodes them. **"Founder A / Founder B"** mean the first and second founders the
organization has defined.

---

## Founder Identity Intake (the questionnaire for adapting this skill)

Ask as one numbered list, **per founder**, and **WAIT** for answers; then record them wherever
your team keeps founder decisions (a list of objects, one per founder). Ask only what's
missing. Never invent any of these — they describe a real person.

For each founder:

1. **Name** — full name as it should appear publicly?
2. **Role** — one line (e.g. "the expert who builds", "the strategist who scales")?
3. **Angle** — the distinct lens that sets them apart from the other founder(s)?
4. **Background** — career history / domains (employers, ventures) to draw stories from?
5. **Core message** — the single gap or problem they're known for naming?
6. **Content tone** — how they sound (e.g. methodology-driven vs. ROI-driven)?
7. **Audience** — who they primarily speak to (titles / segments)?
8. **Voice adjectives** — 3-5 words (e.g. reflective, bold, visionary)?
9. **Credibility line** — the one-line proof of authority they can use?
10. **Stage pitch** — their one-line keynote/stage pitch (the talk they'd give)?
11. **Pillars** — their content-pillar split, or use the skill's default 35/30/20/15?
12. **Strategy docs** — where their branding doc and GTM/schedule doc live (path or link)?

Also capture once for the org:

- **Marketing-philosophy doc** — where the content idea bank and core/personality/wildcard
  mix live?
- **Brand sign-off line** — the short company close used on collab posts (from the company's
  brand voice)?

> **Privacy note**: founder bios, personal stories, and credentials are private to the org.
> Keep them wherever your team stores founder decisions, never in the shared skill.

## Founder Voice Validation Checklist

```markdown
## Voice Check — [Founder Name] — [Post Topic]

### Identity Check
- [ ] Uses "I" (personal), not "we" (company voice)
- [ ] Matches the founder's defined angle (not the other founder's)
- [ ] References the founder's specific, real experience (not generic)
- [ ] Doesn't overlap with the other founder's content this week

### Quality Bar
- [ ] Stage test: would this be credible on a keynote stage?
- [ ] Teaches, inspires, or provokes thought?
- [ ] Only this founder could say this (personal experience)?
- [ ] Premium, on-brand visual quality?

### Cross-Promotion Check
- [ ] References the company naturally (if applicable) — not forced
- [ ] Thematically aligned with the company's campaign this week?
- [ ] Tags co-founder (if collab content)?

### Verdict: [ ] Ready  [ ] Needs voice adjustment  [ ] Wrong founder angle
```

## Founder LinkedIn Growth Checklist (Mandatory)

```markdown
## Founder Growth Check — [Founder] — [Post]

- [ ] Hook is short, sharp, and scroll-stopping
- [ ] Includes one concrete proof point (number/case/pattern) from real experience
- [ ] Explains mechanism (not only opinion)
- [ ] Keeps the founder-specific defined angle
- [ ] Ends with comment-focused CTA ("Agree or disagree?" / "Comment 1/2/3")

### Verdict: [ ] Growth-ready  [ ] Rewrite needed
```

## Founder Intro Authenticity Checklist (Mandatory)

```markdown
## Founder Intro Check — [Founder] — [Post]

- [ ] Includes a first-hand line (e.g., "I've seen...", "What stands out to me...")
- [ ] Includes one concrete, real detail (moment, decision, behavior, context)
- [ ] Avoids generic praise ("visionary", "dynamic", "great leader") without evidence
- [ ] Reads naturally when spoken aloud
- [ ] Feels like a human relationship, not a press release
- [ ] Preserves the founder-specific defined tone

### Verdict: [ ] Founder-authentic  [ ] Rewrite needed
```

```markdown
### Setup
**Question**: [The debatable question]

**Founder B's Position**: [1-line stance — from their angle]

### Founder A's Post
**Platform**: [LinkedIn / X]
**Angle**: [Founder A's defined angle]
**Copy**:
[Founder A's post text — ends by tagging Founder B and inviting the audience to pick sides]

### Founder B's Post (Same Day)
**Platform**: [LinkedIn / X]
**Angle**: [Founder B's defined angle]
**Copy**:
[Founder B's post text — references Founder A's take, presents the counterargument, tags Founder A]

### Rules Verified
- [ ] Not scripted — authentic disagreement
- [ ] Both tag each other
- [ ] Playful energy, not corporate
- [ ] Ends with the brand sign-off line (from the company's brand voice)
- [ ] Audience invited to participate
```

## Founder Differentiation Quick Reference

When writing, always verify you're in the right voice. Build this table from the two
founders' defined angles — fill each cell with how *that* founder would
respond to the signal, in their own lens. The examples below show the *pattern* (practitioner
vs. strategist) using neutral placeholders, not real founder lines.

| Signal | Founder A (e.g. practitioner lens) | Founder B (e.g. strategist lens) |
|--------|------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Quality/foundation issue | "Fix the foundation before adding the polish" | "What's the bottom-line impact of the broken foundation?" |
| Tech adoption | "Start with the question, not the technology" | "Start with the highest-value decision" |
| Client story | "Same gap across every org I've worked in" | "Every company I've evaluated had this blind spot" |
| Output critique | "Nobody actually used it" | "Show me the return. Not the artifact." |
| Advice | "Build useful first, polish later" | "If it doesn't show a number in 90 days, kill it" |
| Vulnerability | "My most polished work was my most useless" | "My first venture failed" |

> Replace each row with the founders' actual signature lines once captured — keeping each
> founder's distinct fingerprint and never letting the two read interchangeably.
