# Founder Brand Writer

Writes content in the voice of an organization's individual founders, keeping each
founder's angle distinct and complementary — never overlapping. Handles solo posts,
co-founder collaborations, and cross-promotion with the company brand. This skill is the
**method**. The specifics it operates on — the founders' names, angles, voices, pillars,
and the company's brand voice — are an organization's, not part of the skill. See
[`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md) for what to decide before using this for a given organization.

> Two voices, one brand. Each founder should sound like a human author, not assembled by AI.

When the founder and brand specifics are not yet decided, **do not invent them** — produce a
clearly-labeled **GENERIC DRAFT** with neutral `PLACEHOLDER` values (e.g. "Founder A",
"[ANGLE]", "[METRIC]") and list what's missing. **Never invent** founder names, bios,
personal stories, credentials, employers, or the company brand voice — a founder's lived
experience must come from the organization or the founder themselves.

Throughout this skill, **"Founder A"** and **"Founder B"** mean the first and second founders
the organization has defined. Resolve each founder's real name, angle, background, and voice
from those decisions. If the org has more than two founders, apply the same complementary
logic pairwise.

## Content pillar distribution

Use each founder's own pillar split if they have defined one. If a founder has not defined
one, use this neutral default split and confirm it with the user.

| Pillar | Default % | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Founder journey & vision | 35% | Career pivots, failures, co-founder dynamic, day-in-the-life |
| Domain thought leadership | 30% | Contrarian takes, myth-busters, frameworks, debates |
| Story-based solutions | 20% | Mini-dramas, transformation hooks, pattern recognition |
| Leadership, events & wildcards | 15% | Off-topic, recommendations, travel, humor, mentorship |

Tailor each pillar's *focus* to the founder's angle (e.g. a strategy founder's "thought
leadership" leans to ROI cases; a practitioner founder's leans to method and frameworks).

## Content mix

Across all pillars, balance a core / personality / wildcard mix. Use the ratios from the
organization's marketing philosophy doc if defined; otherwise default 60 / 30 / 10.

| Mix | Default % | Meaning |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| Core | 60% | On-domain authority and value |
| Personality | 30% | Human, behind-the-scenes, opinions |
| Wildcard | 10% | Off-topic, experimental, surprising |

## Co-founder collaboration content

When the org has two or more founders, the multi-founder dynamic is a content advantage. Use
it deliberately.

### Collaboration formats

| Format | Concept | Suggested frequency |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| **The Debate** | Both disagree publicly — each posts their side, audience picks | ~1×/month |
| **Split-Screen Reel** | Same question, two answers, each from their angle | ~2×/month |
| **Joint Podcast** | Both as guests — banter plus real debate | ~1×/month |
| **Tag-Team Thread** | One starts on X, the other quote-tweets with their angle | ~2×/month |
| **Behind-the-Scenes** | "Building a company with someone who thinks differently" | ~1×/month |

### Collaboration rules

- **Never scripted** — authentic disagreement is the magic.
- **Tag each other** — cross-pollinates audiences.
- **Keep it playful** — "respectful roast" energy, not corporate debate.
- **Close on the brand** — end with a short company sign-off built from the company's brand
  voice (e.g. a one-line "two perspectives, one mission" tied to the company name). Use the
  company's exact tagline only as written.

## Cross-promotion with the company brand

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Founders share + comment on every company post | Amplifies reach |
| Company reposts founders' best posts occasionally | Builds founder credibility |
| Reference the company naturally | "At [company], we see this pattern…" — never forced |
| Thematic alignment | When the company runs a campaign, founders align that week |

> **Brand-proof identity**: Founders embody the company's brand proof, expressed through the
> company's brand voice attributes and its positioning promise. Their content should
> *demonstrate* the brand's edge through stories and specifics, not state it as a slogan.
> Example pattern: show a concrete result that proves the promise rather than naming the
> promise. Resolve the actual proof points and tagline from the organization's brand
> decisions.

## Quality bar (every founder, every piece)

Every piece of founder content must pass:

1. **Stage test**: Would this be credible from a keynote stage? → If no, raise the bar.
2. **Teach / inspire / provoke**: Does it do at least one? → If no, don't post.
3. **Only they can say this**: Is it personal to *their* experience? → If no, make it personal.
4. **Premium look**: Does the visual look premium and on-brand? → If no, redesign.

## Founder human-voice policy (mandatory)

Founder content must feel authored by a human, not assembled by AI. Enforce on every founder
post and any founder-introduction post:

1. **Human-first draft** — start from founder-originated input (voice note, rough bullets,
   DM-style thoughts), not a cold AI draft.
2. **Personal proof** — include at least one lived moment, decision, or behavior that only
   close collaborators would know. Source it from the organization or the founder; never invent it.
3. **Natural imperfection** — keep conversational rhythm; do not over-polish every sentence.
4. **No bio-style fluff** — ban generic praise ("visionary leader", "dynamic", "great
   leader") unless followed by concrete evidence.
5. **Distinct founder fingerprint** — preserve each founder's signature tone; the two must
   never read interchangeably.

## Founder growth-copy rules (mandatory for LinkedIn)

Apply when writing growth-oriented founder posts:

1. **Open hard** — short first line with tension, a claim, or a contradiction.
2. **Use a proof stack** — at least one metric, case snapshot, or specific observed pattern
   (drawn from the founder's real experience, not fabricated).
3. **Show mechanism** — explain "how it works" in simple steps, not theory only.
4. **Comment CTA required** — every LinkedIn post ends with a comment-focused prompt.
5. **No cold-chase framing** — prefer inbound authority, trust-building, and warm
   conversations over hard outreach.

### Comment CTA defaults

- "Agree or disagree?"
- "Comment 1/2/3 and I'll share what to fix first."
- "What's your biggest blocker right now?"

## Anti-patterns (every founder)

| Never | Instead |
|-------|---------|
| Generic motivational quotes | Personal stories with specific lessons |
| AI-polished founder introductions | Human, first-hand narratives with specific moments |
| LinkedIn posts without a CTA | Add a comment-focused CTA ending every time |
| "We're hiring!" as content | Culture showcases |
| Engagement bait | Genuine questions |
| Hard-selling company services | Soft CTAs — let expertise sell |
| Overlapping content between founders | Complementary angles always |
| Low-quality visuals | Premium, on-brand design |
| Inventing a founder's background or numbers | Pull lived detail from the org or the founder |
