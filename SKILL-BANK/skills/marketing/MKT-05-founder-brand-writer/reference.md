# Founder Brand Writer — Reference

Loaded on demand for voice comparison, content examples, and collaboration patterns. The
example copy below is illustrative and uses neutral placeholders — resolve real founder
names, angles, stories, and numbers from the organization's founder decisions and never
fabricate them. **"Founder A / Founder B"** mean the first and second founders the
organization has defined.

---

## Growth post pattern (high-engagement, human)

Use this structure for founder growth posts:

1. **Hook** — strong claim / contrarian line
2. **Pain frame** — what most people are doing wrong
3. **Mechanism** — 3-4 clear steps / how the system works
4. **Proof** — one metric, case result, or first-hand example (from the founder's real
   experience)
5. **CTA** — comment-focused ending

### Example CTA endings

- "Agree or disagree?"
- "Comment 1/2/3 — I'll share what to fix first."
- "What are you seeing in your company right now?"

### Comment reply style (founder-human)

Keep replies short, warm, and specific:

- "Appreciate this — exactly the point."
- "100%. We've seen the same pattern."
- "Great point. This is where most teams get stuck."
- "Thanks for sharing this. Spot on."

Avoid robotic lines like:

- "Thank you for your valuable comment."
- "Excited to connect and collaborate."

## Voice comparison: same topic, two founders

The power of two founders is two genuinely different lenses on the same topic. The structure
below shows how to write the *same* idea twice. Replace the bracketed placeholders with the
founder's real angle, domain, and lived proof from the organization — keep the lenses distinct.

### Topic: "[A common failure mode in your domain]"

**Founder A's version** (e.g. practitioner / method-driven angle):
```
The worst [project type] I've ever seen cost [$AMOUNT] and produced [N artifacts].

Nobody used them.

Not because the work was wrong. Not because the tools were bad.
Because nobody asked: "What decision will this change on Monday morning?"

I've seen this [across the orgs in my background]. Same gap. Every single time.

The [artifacts] answered the team's questions. Not the leader's.

Here's what I do now before building anything:
→ Sit in 3 meetings. Just listen.
→ Ask: "What keeps you up at night?"
→ Build for THAT answer. Not the one in the requirements doc.

[Artifacts] aren't art. They're decision tools.
If nobody uses it Monday morning — it's decoration.
```

**Founder B's version** (e.g. strategist / ROI-driven angle):
```
A company I [advise/evaluated] spent [$AMOUNT] on [the same thing].

I asked the [decision-maker]: "What was the return?"
They said: "We have better visibility now."
I said: "Show me one decision that changed because of it."

Silence.

[$AMOUNT]. [N months]. [N artifacts]. Zero impact anyone could name.

This is the problem I keep seeing — companies invest in [domain] tools
like they invest in office furniture. It's a line item. Not a strategy.

The question isn't "should we invest?" It's "what's the cost if we don't?"

That's the business case. And until someone frames it that way,
the [artifacts] keep being expensive wallpaper.
```

**What's different**:
- Founder A: method-focused, "here's what I do", question-first approach.
- Founder B: ROI-focused, "here's the business cost", returns framing.
- Same story, completely different lens.

> Resolve `[$AMOUNT]`, employers, and numbers from the founder's defined background or the
> founder's own input. Never invent a dollar figure or a place a founder worked.

## Voice comparison: same topic, different energy

### Topic: "[A hyped trend in your domain]"

**Founder A** (technical / practitioner):
```
Unpopular opinion: most companies don't need [the hyped trend].

They need someone who actually understands their current [system].

I'm serious. Before you spend [$AMOUNT] on a "[trend] strategy" —
can you answer these 3 questions:
→ What's your [quality metric] right now?
→ When did someone last clean up your [core asset]?
→ Do you know which [outputs] nobody uses?

[The trend] amplifies what you already have.
If what you have is a mess — it gives you a faster mess.

Fix the foundation. Then add the [trend].
```

**Founder B** (strategist):
```
Every board meeting this year has the same agenda item:
"Our [trend] strategy."

I sit in these meetings. Here's what I've noticed:

Companies that succeed all have one thing in common —
they didn't start with [the trend]. They started with a question:
"Where are we losing money because of bad decisions?"

Then they worked backward.

The companies that fail? They start with the technology.
"Let's implement [the trend]." Cool. For what? "To be innovative."
That's not a strategy. That's a press release.

If your [trend] investment can't show me a real number
within 90 days, it's not an investment. It's hope.
```

## Co-founder debate example

The debate format pairs two posts on the same day, each in the founder's own voice, tagging
the other. The example below uses neutral placeholders — fill the positions and proof from
the founders' defined angles and lived experience.

### Debate topic: "[A genuinely two-sided question in your domain]"

**Founder A's post**:
```
Hot take: [Founder A's stance, stated plainly].

I've seen [the failure that motivates this stance].
[The cliché it maps to] isn't a cliché — it's the #1 reason
[these projects] fail.

[Concrete consequence of getting it wrong, in 2-3 short lines]:
→ [specific failure detail 1]
→ [specific failure detail 2]
→ [specific failure detail 3]

[Founder A's prescription].

@[Founder B] disagrees. They think there's a case for doing it differently.
I think they're wrong. What do you think? 👇
```

**Founder B's post** (same day):
```
My co-founder @[Founder A] says: "[Founder A's stance]."

They're not wrong. But they're not right either.

Here's the business reality: if you wait for [the ideal precondition],
you'll be waiting forever. Your competitors won't.

The real question isn't "[Founder A's question]?"
It's "what's the cost of waiting [timeframe] to start?"

My approach:
→ [Founder B's step 1 — focused, measurable]
→ [Founder B's step 2 — start small on the highest-value slice]
→ [Founder B's step 3 — use early results to build the case for the rest]

Perfection is the enemy of progress.

@[Founder A]'s right about one thing: [the point of agreement].
But you don't need everything perfect to start. You need one [unit].

Who's with me? Who's with [Founder A]? 👇
```

## Founder-specific hooks

Keep a per-founder hook bank derived from the org's content idea bank (the marketing
philosophy doc). For each founder, adapt a shared hook into *their*
voice. Build these tables from real material; the rows below show the adaptation *pattern*.

### Founder A hooks (e.g. method-driven adaptation)

| Generic hook | Founder A adaptation (pattern) |
|--------------|--------------------------------|
| "[N artifacts]" story | "Same gap across [their orgs] — the question was always the problem" |
| "Worst [thing] I ever built" | "It was beautiful. It was useless. Here's what it taught me." |
| "[Domain] is dead" | "Not dead. Just badly built. The tool was never the problem." |
| Pattern recognition | "Whether it's a giant or a 10-person startup — same gap." |
| Question-first | "A client asked for [N] metrics. I asked: 'What decision will change Monday?'" |

### Founder B hooks (e.g. ROI-driven adaptation)

| Generic hook | Founder B adaptation (pattern) |
|--------------|--------------------------------|
| "ROI of [trend]" | "The ROI of [trend] is a lie — unless you measure it like an accountant." |
| "[$AMOUNT] investment" | "As an investor, I would have walked. Here's what the [signal] told me." |
| "[Maturity metric]" | "[Maturity] is the new due diligence. If you can't show me this, I question everything." |
| Business case | "If your [decision-maker] can't read your [artifact], it's not an [artifact]." |
| Investor lens | "What I look for in a company's [maturity] before investing." |

## Founder intro pattern (human-first)

Use this pattern for "Meet [Founder]" style posts:

1. **Personal opening** — "Meet [Name] — my co-founder at [company]."
2. **Observed trait + proof** — mention one behavior you have actually observed.
3. **Why it matters to company-building** — link the trait to execution / culture / outcomes.
4. **Forward line** — invite the audience to hear directly from the founder in coming posts.

Avoid this pattern:
- Bio summary + generic praise + corporate tone.

Prefer this pattern:
- First-hand voice + specific detail + natural rhythm.
