# Adapting Brand Guidelines

**What this skill does:** applies one organization's visual identity (colors, type, logo,
tagline, voice) to designed assets and reviews work for brand compliance.

This skill is generic methodology — it never hardcodes a specific brand. To use it for
your organization, decide the values below and either keep them in your design brief at
use time, or bake them into your forked copy of `SKILL.md`. Until they're decided, the
skill emits a labeled GENERIC DRAFT rather than inventing a brand.

## What to decide for your organization

| Decide | Used in `SKILL.md` under | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Palette** — primary, accent, light base, dark base (name + hex each) | Color discipline | Note if the accent is "use sparingly". |
| **Semantic colors** — success / warning / error / info | Color discipline | Defaults are fine if you have none. |
| **Typefaces** — display/headline font, mono/technical font, optional accent font | Typography | State a system-font fallback. |
| **Logo** — asset location, light-on-dark vs dark-on-light variants | Logo usage | — |
| **Clear space & minimum size** — padding rule + smallest allowed size | Logo usage | — |
| **Tagline & wordmark** — exact strings incl. punctuation | Tagline & messaging | Never modify or translate the tagline. |
| **Descriptor** — one positioning line (optional) | Tagline & messaging | — |
| **Off-limits** — colors, fonts, clichés to avoid | Tagline & messaging, Design quality | — |
| **Contrast target** — minimum ratio (default WCAG AA 4.5:1) | Accessibility | Raise to AAA where required. |

## How to gather these

The numbered **Brand Intake** in [`forms.md`](forms.md) is the questionnaire for all of the
above — ask it once, record the answers wherever your team keeps brand decisions.

## Platform-independent (do not change)

`forms.md` checklists and `reference.md` platform dimensions are brand-neutral facts —
they work as-is for any organization.
