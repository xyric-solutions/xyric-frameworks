# Adapting ICP & UCP Targeting

**What this skill does:** targets every piece of marketing — content, messaging, lead
qualification, campaigns — to a specific Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) tier and reinforces at
least one Unique Competitive Positioning (UCP) pillar.

This skill is generic methodology — it never hardcodes a specific audience or positioning. To
use it for your organization, decide the values below and either keep them in your campaign /
content brief at use time, or bake them into your forked copy of `SKILL.md`. Until they're
decided, the skill emits a labeled GENERIC DRAFT (neutral `Tier A / Tier B` placeholders)
rather than inventing tiers, pillars, messages, the promise, or pricing.

## What to decide for your organization

| Decide | Used in `SKILL.md` under | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Brand promise** — one-line core value statement | Key messages | The north star every message bridges back to. |
| **ICP tiers** — named audience segments, each with who/roles, firmographics (size/stage/industry), and core pain | ICP: the audience axis; Messaging by tier | **No pricing** — keep tiers about who and pain. |
| **Shared psychographics** — traits common to all tiers (mindset, triggers, what they're allergic to) | ICP: the audience axis | Adapt the generic frame in `reference.md`. |
| **ICP anti-patterns** — audiences you deliberately do NOT serve, and why | ICP: the audience axis; Anti-Patterns | Used to qualify leads out early. |
| **UCP pillars** — competitive-positioning pillars, each a name + one-line meaning + concrete proof point | UCP: the differentiation axis | Fall back to your differentiators if you have no formal pillars. |
| **Differentiators** — what makes you different (fallback pillar set) | UCP: the differentiation axis | Only needed if you lack named UCP pillars. |
| **Key messages** — 5–9 canonical, pre-approved messages, each with its best context | Key messages | Used verbatim, including punctuation. |
| **Per-tier messaging** — for each tier, the message that resonates vs. the one that falls flat | Messaging by tier | The off-key register to avoid per tier. |
| **Tier coverage targets** (optional) — desired % of content per tier per quarter | (coverage audit, `forms.md`) | Optional; informs the quarterly audit. |

## How to gather these

The numbered **Positioning Intake** in [`forms.md`](forms.md) is the questionnaire for all of
the above — ask it once, record the answers wherever your team keeps positioning decisions.

## Platform-independent (do not change)

`forms.md` (Content Targeting Form, Lead Qualification Checklist, Campaign ICP Mapping,
Quarterly Coverage Audit, decision tree) and `reference.md` (tier profile frame, shared
psychographic frame, messaging-by-tier discipline, pillar-reinforcement frame) are method
scaffolding — universal frames you fill with your own values. They work as-is for any
organization.
