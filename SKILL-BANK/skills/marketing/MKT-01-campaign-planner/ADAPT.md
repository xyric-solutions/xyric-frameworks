# Adapting Campaign Planner

**What this skill does:** plans audience-centric marketing campaigns and multi-week GTM
plans organized around the customer's pain points, not the organization's offerings.

This skill is generic methodology — it never hardcodes a specific organization's audience,
messaging, channels, or offerings. To use it for your organization, decide the values below
and either keep them in your campaign brief at use time, or bake them into your forked copy
of `SKILL.md`. Until they're decided, the skill emits a labeled GENERIC DRAFT (with
`PLACEHOLDER` values) rather than inventing pain points, audience tiers, messaging,
differentiators, channels, founders, or metrics.

## What to decide for your organization

| Decide | Used in `SKILL.md` under | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Brand promise** — one core value statement | Campaign Philosophy, Positioning principles | The through-line for all campaigns. |
| **Key messages** — 5–9 statements you want associated with you | Audience Targeting, Differentiators | One lead message per ICP tier / pain point. |
| **Customer pain points / differentiators** — the 3–6 problems your offering solves, plus credible proof points | Pain-Point Campaign Framework, Differentiators in Campaigns | These become campaign themes; never fabricate metrics or guarantees. |
| **ICP tiers** — audience segments, one-line description each | Audience Targeting for Campaigns | No pricing; do not invent tier names. |
| **Brand voice** — tone attributes (3–5 adjectives) + an "avoid" list | Messaging Principles, Campaign Philosophy | Drives phrasing; nothing from the avoid list may appear. |
| **Content pillars** — 3–5 recurring themes | Channel Strategy (content mix) | Feeds the 60/30/10 Core split. |
| **Active channels** — where you publish (professional network, short-form video, micro-posts, owned content, audio) | Channel Strategy, GTM cadence tables | Drives the channel rows and per-channel cadence. |
| **Founders / personal brands** — names + angles, if founder content is in the mix | Continuation context, Related skill roles | One GTM plan stream per founder. |
| **Business model** — one line (consultancy, SaaS, marketplace, …) | Positioning principles (partner framing) | Frames the partner-vs-vendor positioning. |
| **Service lines / offerings** — list mapped to pain points behind the scenes | Pain-Point Campaign Framework | Internal mapping only; mentioned only after problem relevance. |
| **Posting volume** — default cadence if different from the heuristics | GTM Plan Generation, cadence tables | The numeric cadences are defaults; override with yours. |
| **GTM plan storage location** — where existing plans live | Continuation context check | Needed to detect Continuation mode and avoid theme/format repeats. |

## How to gather these

The numbered **Campaign Context Intake** in [`forms.md`](forms.md) is the questionnaire for
all of the above — ask it once, record the answers wherever your team keeps campaign and
positioning decisions.

## Platform-independent (do not change)

The cadence curves, funnel shapes, theme-escalation arc, content calendars, campaign-type
deep-dives, and all checklists/templates in [`forms.md`](forms.md) and
[`reference.md`](reference.md) are generic campaign mechanics — they work as-is for any
organization. Only the values in the table above are organization-specific.
