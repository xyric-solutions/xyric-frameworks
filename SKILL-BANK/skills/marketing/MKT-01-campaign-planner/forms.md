# Campaign Planner — Forms & Checklists

Ready-to-use intake, output templates, and quality checklists. The audience, messaging,
channel, and offering specifics are an organization's decisions (see
[`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md)); this file never hardcodes them.

---

## Campaign Context Intake (the questionnaire for adapting this skill)

This is the questionnaire that gathers the organization-specific values in `ADAPT.md`. Ask
it as one numbered list and **WAIT** for answers; then record the answers wherever your team
keeps campaign and positioning decisions. Ask only what's missing.

**Positioning & audience**:
1. **Brand promise** — one core value statement?
2. **Key messages** — 5–9 statements you want the market to associate with you?
3. **Customer pain points** — the 3–6 problems your offering solves (these become campaign themes)?
4. **ICP tiers** — your audience segments, with a one-line description each (no pricing)?
5. **Differentiators / proof points** — what makes you different, and any credible outcome claims (timelines, quality, results) you can cite?

**Voice**:
6. **Tone attributes** — 3–5 adjectives for how campaign copy should sound?
7. **Avoid list** — words, clichés, or claims that are off-limits?

**Content & channels**:
8. **Content pillars** — your 3–5 recurring themes?
9. **Active channels** — where you publish (professional network, short-form video, micro-posts, owned content, audio)?
10. **Founders / personal brands** — names and angles, if founder content is part of the mix?

**Business**:
11. **Business model** — one line (e.g. consultancy, SaaS, marketplace)?
12. **Service lines / offerings** — the list you map to pain points behind the scenes?

## Campaign Planning Checklist

### Phase 1: Problem Definition
```
- [ ] Identify a specific customer pain point (from the org's positioning)
- [ ] Quantify the cost of the problem
- [ ] Map to relevant offerings from the org's service lines (internal reference only)
- [ ] Define our unique perspective (the org's differentiators)
- [ ] Reference documented methodology if applicable
```

### Phase 2: Audience Understanding
```
- [ ] Define who experiences this problem (the org's ICP tiers)
- [ ] Identify their role and industry
- [ ] Understand their current solutions (and frustrations)
- [ ] Map where they consume content (the org's active channels)
```

### Phase 3: Content Strategy
```
- [ ] Define the campaign theme and angle
- [ ] Plan the post series (5–7 posts)
- [ ] Plan long-form content (1–2 pieces)
- [ ] Ensure voice consistency (the org's brand voice)
- [ ] Plan visuals per the brand skill / the org's brand
```

### Phase 4: Execution
```
- [ ] Create a content calendar
- [ ] Develop content assets
- [ ] Schedule posts per channel
- [ ] Publish long-form content
- [ ] Monitor and engage with responses
```

### Phase 5: Measurement
```
- [ ] Track engagement metrics
- [ ] Monitor discovery-call requests
- [ ] Document what resonated
- [ ] Capture learnings for the next campaign
```

## Content Calendar Framework

Map the day columns to the org's active channels; the structure below is generic.

### Weekly Rhythm (Full Cadence — Week 3+)

| Day | Primary channel | Micro-post channel | Video channel |
|-----|-----------------|--------------------|---------------|
| **Monday** | Opening insight + campaign post | Hot take / one-liner | — |
| **Tuesday** | Culture / BTS / team series | Thread or engagement | Story content |
| **Wednesday** | Methodology / contrarian | Debate starter | — |
| **Thursday** | Story hook / case study | Quick take | Clip (from podcast if available) |
| **Friday** | Engagement Q / vulnerable / wildcard | Week wrap + reflective | Clip (trending or brand) |

### Weekly Rhythm (Launch Mode — Week 1 only)

| Day | Primary | Secondary |
|-----|---------|-----------|
| **Monday** | Manifesto / launch story | — |
| **Tuesday** | Culture / team series | — |
| **Wednesday** | Contrarian take | — |
| **Thursday** | Poll or hot take | — |
| **Friday** | Short intro video | — |

### Monthly Rhythm

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Week 1 | Campaign theme introduction |
| Week 2 | Problem deep-dive |
| Week 3 | Methodology / approach |
| Week 4 | Success patterns + month wrap-up |

### Quarterly Planning
```
- [ ] Select the pain-point focus for the quarter (from the org's positioning)
- [ ] Plan 2–3 major content pieces
- [ ] Define content-series themes
- [ ] Align with business priorities (specify, or pull from the org's planning docs)
- [ ] Review previous-quarter performance
- [ ] Review the cumulative format tracker — rotate in underused formats
- [ ] Identify new formats to introduce (carousels, newsletters, collabs)
- [ ] Set KPI targets relative to previous-quarter actuals
```

## Campaign Launch Readiness Checklist
```
- [ ] Pain point clearly defined and quantified
- [ ] Content aligned with the org's brand voice
- [ ] Visual assets follow the org's brand / brand skill
- [ ] Post series planned (5–7 posts)
- [ ] Long-form content drafted
- [ ] Discovery-call pathway clear
```

## Voice Alignment Checklist
```
- [ ] Customer problem is the headline, not the org
- [ ] Educational value before any ask
- [ ] Partner positioning, not vendor
- [ ] Specific outcomes, not vague benefits
- [ ] Methodology / differentiator referenced where relevant
- [ ] Nothing from the org's brand voice "avoid" list present
```

## Quality Gates
```
- [ ] Reviewed for anti-patterns
- [ ] Unconfirmed data marked PLACEHOLDER for later confirmation
- [ ] No service-catalog language
- [ ] No hype without substance
- [ ] All metrics/claims sourced from the org's differentiators, not invented
```
