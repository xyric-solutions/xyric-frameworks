# Adapting Brand Voice Guardian

**What this skill does:** guards one organization's written voice — tagline, voice
attributes, banned/preferred words, key messages — so every external piece sounds
consistent and distinct, and reviews existing copy against that voice.

This skill is generic methodology — it never hardcodes a specific brand's voice. To use
it for your organization, decide the values below and either keep them in your content
brief at use time, or bake them into your forked copy of `SKILL.md`. Until they're
decided, the skill emits a labeled GENERIC DRAFT rather than inventing a voice.

## What to decide for your organization

| Decide | Used in `SKILL.md` under | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Voice attributes** — the 3-5 adjectives that define how you sound | What "on-brand voice" means; Voice Alignment checklist | e.g. insightful, practical, confident. |
| **Tagline** — exact string incl. punctuation | Brand promise comes through in tone; Anti-patterns | Used verbatim — never modify, abbreviate, or translate. |
| **Brand promise** — one positioning line the voice must live up to | Brand promise comes through in tone | — |
| **Key messages** — the 5-9 points the voice should reinforce | Both sides of the offer; Brand Consistency | Capture every side of the offer, not just the obvious one. |
| **Banned phrases** — words/phrases you never use (beyond the generic dead-word list) | Language discipline; Dead words | Appended to the universal kill list at review time. |
| **Preferred phrases** — on-brand replacements for transactional/buzzword language | Language discipline | The swap-in words. |
| **Off-limits clichés** — anything else to never say | Language discipline | — |
| **Relationship framing** — service/partner org ("we're your team") vs. product org | Partnership / relationship voice | Shapes which framing table applies. |
| **Founder / personal accounts** — anyone who writes under a personal name distinct from the company | Distinct voices by account | For each: name, angle, voice. Omit if no personal accounts. |

## How to gather these

The numbered **Voice Intake** in [`forms.md`](forms.md) is the questionnaire for all of
the above — ask it once, record the answers wherever your team keeps brand decisions.

## Platform-independent (do not change)

`forms.md` checklists/templates (Voice Checklist, Content Review, Audit, Transformation
Checklist) and the universal dead-words / buzzword kill lists and worked transformations
in `reference.md` are brand-neutral techniques — they work as-is for any organization.
Only the organization's own attributes, tagline, and word lists get filled in.
