# Adapting Client Communication

**What this skill does:** drafts and reviews professional, personalized client emails that
read like an embedded partner — progress updates, deliverables, meetings, blockers,
follow-ups, scope changes, and handovers.

This skill is generic methodology — it never hardcodes a sender, voice, or positioning. To
use it for your organization, decide the values below and either keep them in your brief at
use time, or bake them into your forked copy of `SKILL.md`. Until they're decided, the skill
emits a labeled GENERIC DRAFT (with `[Sender]`, `[Your Org]` placeholders) rather than
inventing them.

## What to decide for your organization

| Decide | Used in `SKILL.md` under | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Sender name** — the name (or initial) that signs the emails | Tone adaptation guide; `[Sender]` in all templates | Use the signing individual's name where a person signs. |
| **Descriptor** — one-line "what we are" for sign-offs / About lines | Tone adaptation guide; templates | Optional. |
| **Brand voice attributes** — 3-5 adjectives the writing embodies | Partnership voice; Tone adaptation guide | E.g. clear, confident, practical. |
| **Off-limits phrasing** — words / clichés to never use | AI-cringe anti-patterns | Adds to the built-in anti-pattern list. |
| **Partnership promise** — the core value statement you frame work around | Core philosophy; Partnership voice | Shapes how value is framed. |
| **Differentiators** — what makes the organization different | Partnership voice; Integration references | Reinforced subtly in messaging. |
| **Response-time standard** — committed turnaround, if any | Response time guidelines | Falls back to the defaults if none. |

## How to gather these

The numbered **Communication Intake** in [`forms.md`](forms.md) is the questionnaire for all
of the above — ask it once, record the answers wherever your team keeps these decisions.

## Platform-independent (do not change)

`forms.md` (the Quality Checklist and the 10 email templates) and `reference.md` (tone
adaptation guides, difficult-situation patterns, the full AI-cringe anti-pattern list) are
universal email-craft facts — they work as-is for any organization. Only the
sender/voice/positioning placeholders inside the templates get filled in.
