# Adapting Cold Outreach

**What this skill does:** writes hook-first, scannable, results-backed cold outreach emails
for an organization's service lines or products, and reviews drafts against a human/dead-words
check before sending.

This skill is generic methodology — it never hardcodes a specific organization. To use it
for yours, decide the values below and either keep them in your outreach brief at draft
time, or bake them into your forked copy of `SKILL.md`, `forms.md`, and `reference.md`.
Until they're decided, the skill emits a labeled GENERIC DRAFT with neutral placeholders
(`[ORG NAME]`, `[ONE-LINER]`, `[DIFFERENTIATOR]`) rather than inventing them.

## What to decide for your organization

| Decide | Used in `SKILL.md` under | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Org name & one-liner** — exact name + one-sentence elevator description | The Org Identity in Cold Emails; Sign-off | Used in the "who we are" block and the sign-off. |
| **Descriptor** — the one-line "what we are" that sits under the tagline | The Org Identity in Cold Emails | — |
| **Tagline** — exact string, including punctuation | The Org Identity in Cold Emails | Use verbatim; never modify or translate. |
| **Voice attributes** — 3-5 adjectives for how the org sounds | The 5 Rules (Human voice); Human Check | Drives the "sounds human, not generic agency" check. |
| **Words to avoid** — phrases/clichés the org never uses | Dead Words in Cold Emails | Added to the kill list alongside the universal dead words. |
| **Differentiators** — what sets the org apart, as concrete proof points (speed, ownership, who-you-talk-to, specialization) | The Org Identity; "Who we are in N seconds"; Anti-Patterns | The lead differentiator becomes the speed/proof bullet. |
| **Key messages** — the 5-9 core messages to draw from | Email Structure (personalized value) | Pick 2-3 per email. |
| **ICP tiers** — prospect segments you target + how messaging shifts per tier | Related skills (ICP targeting) | Used to pick angle and pain stat per prospect. |
| **Business model** — one-line model; note if it has more than one "side" (e.g. optimize-existing vs. build-from-zero) | The Org Identity; "Who we are in N seconds" | Pick the side that matches the prospect. |
| **Service lines** — each offering with a one-line summary | Email Structure (who-we-are block); "Who we are in N seconds" | One "who we are" variant per service line. |
| **Sign-off** — who signs cold emails (first name + role) | Email Structure (Sign-off) | First name + role + org name only. |

## How to gather these

The numbered **Cold Outreach Intake** in [`forms.md`](forms.md) is the questionnaire for all
of the above — ask it once, record the answers wherever your team keeps outreach decisions
(an outreach brief, a positioning doc, or your fork of these files).

## Platform-independent (do not change)

The following are universal cold-email craft, neutral to any organization, and work as-is:

- `forms.md` — the Email Brief template, Pre-Send Checklist, Follow-Up Cadence, Follow-Up
  Templates, and Monthly Outreach Tracker.
- `reference.md` — the five template skeletons, subject-line patterns, CTA variations,
  results-discipline guidance, and personalization checklist. Every metric in these
  templates is a bracketed placeholder — fill it from the prospect's own numbers, never
  with fixed boilerplate.
