# Cold Outreach — Reference: Templates & Examples

Loaded on demand for email template skeletons, subject-line patterns, CTA variations, and
the results-discipline guidance. Org-specific values (name, tagline, differentiators,
service lines, sign-off) are decided per organization — see [`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md); the
structures below are universal cold-email craft. **Every metric in these templates is a
bracketed placeholder — fill it from the prospect's own numbers, never with fixed
boilerplate.**

---

## How to read these templates

- `[First Name]`, `[Company]`, `[industry]`, `[process]` → filled per prospect.
- The **"who we are in N seconds"** bullet block → assembled from the org's differentiators
  + the matched service-line entry (see SKILL.md → "Who we are in N seconds — assemble per
  service line"). The bullets shown are *shape* examples, written for a generic data/AI
  service org — replace them with the org's own.
- The **sign-off** → `[First Name]` then role + org name (the org's decided sign-off).
- All `[X]%`, `[X] hours`, `[X] weeks`, `[timeframe]` → computed from the prospect; bracket
  until defensible.

## Template 1: Primary Service Line (The Flagship)

Use for the org's lead service line. The example below is a fully generic data/analytics
scenario — swap the bullets and numbers for the org's own.

```
Subject: Can I save your team [X] hours a week?

Hey [First Name],

I understand [Company] already has a strong [data/analytics] function in place.
Quick question though — how much of your team's time still goes into manually
pulling, cleaning, and consolidating data?

For most [industry] companies your size, it's [X-Y] hours a week. We've cut
that to near-zero for companies like yours in the last [timeframe].

**[Who we are] in [N] seconds:**
- [Lead service-line summary — what we build/optimize, as our specialization]
- [Speed proof — e.g. "First quote in [X] hours. Team onboarded in [X] hours"]
- [Ownership point — e.g. "You own everything — full code, zero lock-in"]
- [Human angle — e.g. "You're talking to a founder, not a sales team"]

**What we'd look at for [Company]:**
- Where your team is spending time on manual [process]
- [Specific automation/opportunity relevant to them]
- Quick wins you could see results from in [X-Y] weeks

**Results we'd target for [Company]:**
- [X]% reduction in [the manual work] — computed from your current hours
- [X-Y] hours/week given back to the team
- First results visible in [timeframe]

[X] minutes of your time — and I'll show you exactly where those [X] hours
are going. When works?

[First Name]
[Role], [Org Name]
```

## Worked Example (fully fictional — illustrates structure only)

This is a made-up scenario for a fictional analytics firm "NorthPeak", offering to a
fictional retailer "Vellora Foods". It shows *how the pieces fit*, not values to reuse. The
real org's name, bullets, and numbers come from the org's decided values and the prospect.

```
Subject: Can I save your team 12 hours a week?

Hey Dana,

I read that Vellora Foods just expanded into two new regions — congrats. With
that kind of growth, how much of your analytics team's week still goes into
manually stitching together sales data from each store?

For most regional retailers your size it's 10-14 hours a week. We'd want to
get that close to zero.

**NorthPeak in 10 seconds:**
- We build and fix data pipelines — that's all we do
- First scope back to you in hours, not weeks
- You keep the full codebase, no lock-in
- You'd work directly with a founder, not a handoff chain

**What we'd look at for Vellora:**
- Where the team is losing time consolidating per-store data
- Automating the regional roll-up so it's one click, not a morning's work
- A quick win you could see inside the first few weeks

**What we'd target for Vellora:**
- ~[X]% of that manual consolidation removed — we'd size it from your real hours
- The hours that buys back, redirected to merchandising analysis
- First results inside [a few] weeks

10 minutes and I'll show you exactly where those hours are going. When works?

Sam
Co-Founder, NorthPeak
```

Note: even in the example, the *result* numbers stay bracketed/qualified. Never present an
unverified percentage as a promise.

## Subject Line Patterns

### Value-First Subjects (Best Performers)
- "Can I save your team [X] hours a week?"
- "Your [process] — [X] days, not [X] months"
- "[Company]'s [asset] — a quick thought"
- "Turning [Company]'s [asset] into a product"

### Curiosity Subjects
- "[First Name] — quick question about [Company]'s [process]"
- "Saw [Company]'s [announcement] — had an idea"
- "The [X] hours [Company] is losing every week"

### Direct Subjects
- "[Company] + [Org] — [service line]"
- "Building [specific thing] for [Company]"

### Subjects to AVOID
- "Partnership Opportunity" — generic, screams spam
- "Introduction — [Org]" — no one opens this
- "[Service] Services" — boring, corporate
- "[First Name], let's connect!" — social-feed energy, not email
- Anything starting with "Exciting" or "Innovative"

## CTA Variations

### Value-Reframe CTAs (Best)
- "[X] minutes of your time — and I'll show you exactly where those [X] hours are going. When works?"
- "[X] minutes — I'll walk you through what we'd build for [Company]. When works?"
- "Got [X] minutes? I'll show you exactly what a [deliverable] looks like."

### Low-Commitment CTAs
- "If this sounds relevant, just reply 'interested' and I'll share specifics for [Company]."
- "Happy to send a 1-page breakdown of what we'd do for [Company]. Want me to?"
- "Not asking for a meeting — just reply if this is on your radar and I'll send specifics."

### Direct CTAs
- "When's a good [X] minutes this week?"
- "Tuesday or Thursday — either work?"

### CTAs to AVOID
- "Would you be available for a quick call?" — passive, weak
- "I'd love to schedule a meeting" — formal, feels like a vendor
- "Let me know if you'd like to discuss" — nobody says "yes" to this
- "Looking forward to hearing from you" — assumption, not a CTA

## Results Discipline (Use in Emails)

**Never ship a fixed metric.** Every figure below is a *slot* you fill by computing from the
prospect's real situation, or by quoting a genuinely comparable engagement you can defend.

### How to build a results bullet
1. Get (or estimate) the prospect's current cost: hours/week, error rate, cycle time, spend.
2. Derive the improvement as a range, not a point claim: "[X-Y]% less manual handling."
3. State the timeframe to first result honestly: "first results in [X] weeks."
4. If you have no defensible figure, use directional language: "companies like yours
   typically see [range]."

### Result slot shapes (fill, never hardcode)
- `[X]% reduction in [the specific manual work]` — derived from their hours
- `[X-Y] hours/week given back to the team`
- `first results in [X] weeks`
- `[X]x faster than [their current path / building in-house]`
- `[deliverable] from concept to live in [X] weeks`

### Speed proof (from the org's differentiators)
If the org's lead differentiator is speed, state it as a concrete proof point pulled from
the org's decided differentiators (e.g. "first scope in [X] hours", "team onboarded in
[X] hours"), with the industry baseline for contrast — but only use baselines you can stand
behind.

> **Important**: Only use results you can back up. Never make up numbers. If a specific
> number doesn't exist yet, use directional language. No invented client names, case
> studies, or product names.
