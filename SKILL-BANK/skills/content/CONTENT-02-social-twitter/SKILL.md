# Social Content - Twitter/X

Creates punchy, authoritative Twitter/X content in one organization's voice:
thought leadership through hot takes, contrarian insights, and real-time industry
commentary. This skill is the **method** — the platform formats and frameworks below are
universal; the organization's actual voice, content pillars, audience tiers, and key
messages are decided per organization. See [`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md) for what to decide before
using this for a given organization.

When the voice, pillars, audience tiers, key messages, or differentiators are not yet
decided, **do not invent them** — produce a clearly-labeled **GENERIC DRAFT** with neutral,
obviously-non-brand placeholder topics and a plain confident tone marked `PLACEHOLDER`, and
list what's missing.

> "Say in a tweet what takes others a blog post. Be the sharpest voice in the room."

## When to Activate

**Triggers**:
- "Create a tweet about [topic]"
- "Write a Twitter thread on [subject]"
- "Help me with X content"
- Reacting to industry news in real-time
- Sharing quick insights or hot takes

**Related skills** (resolve their IDs from the consuming workspace; do not assume a fixed map):
brand-voice skill (voice alignment), campaign/pain-point skill (themes), content-copy
skill (templates adapted for X brevity), founder-brand skill (personal X content), content-
strategy skill (format-diversity check), audience-targeting skill (messaging by tier),
long-form social skill (cross-platform adaptation, not copy-paste).

## Twitter/X Philosophy

> Use the organization's tagline and one-line descriptor as the north star for tone and
> positioning. Mirror the energy of those words — do not paraphrase or replace them.

> "Be the sharpest voice in the room. Equal platform to long-form social — different energy,
> same authority."

### Twitter vs. long-form social

| Long-form (e.g. LinkedIn) | Twitter/X |
|---------------------------|-----------|
| Polished, structured | Punchy, immediate |
| "We've seen this pattern..." | "Hot take:" |
| Organization voice (formal) | Organization voice (direct) |
| Insight posts | One-liners + threads |
| Few posts/week | Daily presence |

### Platform strengths

Twitter is for:
- **Real-time commentary** — react to news before long-form platforms do
- **Contrarian takes** — bold opinions that spark conversation
- **Technical credibility** — your domain community often lives here
- **Building in public** — process insights (without confidential details)

## Content pillars (Twitter-adapted)

Use the same content pillars your organization runs elsewhere — but execute them sharper
for X. For each pillar, draft a one-liner that compresses the long-form thesis into a
scroll-stopping take. Map every post to at least one pillar.

| Step | What to do |
|------|------------|
| 1 | Pick the content pillar this post serves |
| 2 | State the pillar's core thesis in one contrarian sentence |
| 3 | Tie it to one of the organization's key messages and an audience tier |
| 4 | Strip to the sharpest possible phrasing; cut everything non-load-bearing |

> Worked pillar one-liners and thread angles are illustrated generically in
> [`reference.md`](reference.md). Replace their topics with your own content pillars.

## Content Types

### Single Tweets (≤280 chars)

| Type | Purpose | Energy |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Hot Take** | Contrarian POV that sparks discussion | Bold, direct |
| **Insight Drop** | One observation that makes you think | Sharp, concise |
| **Pattern Recognition** | "We keep seeing this..." | Observational |
| **Industry React** | Real-time commentary | Timely, opinionated |
| **Quote Tweet** | Add perspective to others' content | Value-add, not self-promo |

### Threads (multi-tweet)

| Type | Length | Structure |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Hot Take Deep-Dive** | 5-8 tweets | Take → Context → Evidence → Implication |
| **Framework Share** | 4-6 tweets | Problem → Framework → Application |
| **Pattern Story** | 6-10 tweets | Setup → Situation → Solution → Lesson |
| **Industry Analysis** | 5-7 tweets | Trend → What people miss → Reality |

## Hot Takes Framework

| Good Hot Take | Bad Hot Take |
|---------------|--------------|
| Contrarian but defensible | Controversial for attention |
| Based on real experience | Pure opinion without basis |
| Makes you think differently | Obvious or universally agreed |
| Invites discussion | Shuts down conversation |
| Confident, not arrogant | Attacking people or companies |

## Tweet Structure Formulas

- **Observation + Implication**: [What we see] + [What this means]
- **Wrong vs. Right**: Wrong: [Common approach] / Right: [Better approach]
- **Counter-intuitive Truth**: [Bold statement] + [Why it's true]
- **The Reframe**: Everyone talks about [X]. The real issue is [Y].

## Content Diversity

**60/30/10 mix** on X (adjust the percentages if your organization differs):
- **60% Core**: Pillar-aligned hot takes, contrarian insights, methodology drops
- **30% Personality**: Quick reactions, founder/team banter, real talk
- **10% Wildcard**: Off-topic takes, trending reactions, random polls

**Format diversity rule**: Don't tweet the same format back-to-back. Mix hot takes, threads,
polls, reactions, one-liners.

## Dead Words (never use)

Kill on sight — these read as generic AI copy: "Leverage," "Unlock," "Game-changer," "In
today's fast-paced world," "Let's dive in," "Synergy," "Excited to share." Also kill anything
on the organization's own avoid list.

## Anti-Patterns

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Thread bait ("Thread" with no value) | Empty engagement seeking |
| Hashtag stuffing | Desperate reach grab |
| Engagement bait ("Repost if you agree") | Needy, low-quality |
| Fake controversy ("Unpopular opinion: [popular opinion]") | Dishonest framing |
| Ask for follows/RTs | Desperate energy |
| Excessive emojis | Looks amateur |
| Dead Words / avoid-list terms | Instant AI detection |
| Same format twice in a row | Variety drives retention |

## Posting Strategy

- **Launch (early weeks)**: 1-4 posts/week — X is active from day one
- **Ongoing**: Scale to 1-2 tweets/day
- **Threads**: 2-3 per week
- **Hashtags**: 0-2 max per tweet, minimal use
- **Format**: Line breaks for emphasis, sentence fragments, confident tone

### Founder / team content on X

If the organization has named founder/team voices, draft each person's posts in their stated
angle and voice. Use each person's angle and tone to differentiate them; pair them in
tag-team threads where one opens an angle and another adds a complementary one. If there are
no named founder voices, post only in the organization voice.
