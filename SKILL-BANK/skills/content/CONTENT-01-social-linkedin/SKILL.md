# LinkedIn Content

Create professional, authoritative LinkedIn content that establishes thought leadership
through premium positioning, pattern-based insights, and problem-focused storytelling —
never desperate, always valuable. This skill is the **method**; the specifics it operates
on — the organization's brand voice, content pillars, audience tiers, and key messages —
are decided per organization. See [`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md) for what to decide before using
this for a given organization. The LinkedIn format facts below (limits, post types,
structure) are universal.

When those specifics are not yet decided, **do not invent them** — produce a
clearly-labeled **GENERIC DRAFT** with neutral `PLACEHOLDER` values (e.g. `[PILLAR]`,
`[KEY MESSAGE]`, `[AUDIENCE TIER]`) and list what's missing. **Never invent** brand voice,
content pillars, audience tiers, key messages, founder names, or the tagline — resolve
every concrete value (pillar names, hooks, tagline, audience angles, founder voices) from
the organization's decisions, not from your own assumptions.

## Philosophy

> "We are not here to talk about ourselves. We are here to share insights that make people
> think differently."

Lead with the organization's tagline and positioning (its brand promise and
differentiators) only where they sharpen an insight — not as an announcement.

### Premium positioning guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Share insights that demonstrate expertise | Announce services or capabilities |
| Let results speak through patterns | Claim to be "premium" or "leading" |
| Be confident without arrogance | Over-explain credentials |
| Create scarcity through selectivity | Use urgency or FOMO tactics |
| Sound like you don't need the work | Sound like you're chasing business |

### Voice: company page vs founder

- **Company page**: use "we" and "our team" — never "I" or founder names. Institutional
  authority. Apply the brand's voice attributes.
- **Founder posts**: each named founder uses "I" in their own voice/angle, and references
  the organization naturally ("We see this pattern..."). See [`reference.md`](reference.md)
  → Founder Content.

## Content pillars

Every Core post maps to one of the organization's thought-leadership pillars. A pillar is a
recurring theme + core insight the brand owns. Use the organization's defined pillar set; do
not assume a fixed list. If the pillars are not yet decided, ask the intake questions before
drafting Core content. Full pillar expansion (angles + example hooks) lives in
[`reference.md`](reference.md).

## Post types (universal — LinkedIn formats)

| Type | Purpose | Length | When to Use |
|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Insight Post** | Share a pattern or observation | 800–1,200 chars | 2–3×/week |
| **Methodology Post** | Explain an approach | 1,000–1,500 chars | 1×/week |
| **Pattern Story** | Anonymized case study | 1,200–1,800 chars | 1–2×/month |
| **Industry Commentary** | React to trends/news | 600–1,000 chars | As relevant |
| **Company Update** | Milestones (rare) | 500–800 chars | Sparingly |

## Post structure (universal)

```
[Hook: Observation or insight that makes you stop]

[Context: Why this matters — 1-2 sentences]

[Substance: The actual insight or methodology]

[Implication: What this means for the reader]

[Optional: Soft engagement — question or reflection, NOT bait]
```

## Formatting guidelines (universal — LinkedIn facts)

- **Mobile-first**: short paragraphs (1–2 sentences max), generous white space.
- **Emoji**: 0–2 max, strategic only.
- **Hashtags**: 3–4 maximum, always at the END of the post, never inline.
- **No**: bullet-point lists mid-post, ALL-CAPS emphasis, hashtag stuffing.

## Content diversity

Run a recurring content-mix ratio and rotate formats. A common, durable split is the
**60/30/10 mix** every week (these percentages are a widely-used convention, not
org-specific):

- **60% Core**: pillar-aligned — educational, thought leadership, case studies — always
  with a hook, opinion, or story.
- **30% Personality**: human side — behind-the-scenes, hot takes, vulnerable moments, team
  dynamic.
- **10% Wildcard**: off-topic — trending topics, recommendations, polls.

**Format diversity rule**: never post the same format twice in a row. The format catalog is
in [`reference.md`](reference.md).

## Dead words (never use)

Kill on sight: "Leverage," "Unlock," "Game-changer," "In today's fast-paced world,"
"Let's dive in," "Here's the thing," "Synergy," "I'm thrilled to announce," "Excited to
share." Merge this universal list with anything on the organization's own off-limits list.

## Anti-patterns: the blocklist

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|--------------|
| Dramatic ellipses ("And then it hit me...") | LinkedIn cringe, obvious AI |
| Numbered listicles ("5 things I learned:") | Template-feeling, overdone |
| Engagement bait ("Drop a heart if you agree!") | Desperate, low-quality |
| "Let that sink in" | The calling card of AI content |
| Service announcements | Vendor behavior |
| Generic AI marketing speak | Could come from any company |
| Same format twice in a row | Audience fatigue — variety = retention |
| Dead words from the kill list | Instant AI detection |

## Posting cadence

- **Quality over quantity**: skip a day rather than post filler.
- **Best times**: weekday mornings (≈7–10 AM) or midday (≈12–1 PM) in the audience's local
  time zone — adapt to the time zones implied by the organization's audience tiers.
- Set per-channel volume from the organization's active channels and active founders. A
  typical rhythm: company page 3–5 posts/week, each founder 3–5/week.

## When to activate

**Triggers**: "Create a LinkedIn post about [topic]", "Write LinkedIn content for
[announcement]", "Help with LinkedIn thought leadership", sharing insights/patterns/
methodology, milestone announcements.
