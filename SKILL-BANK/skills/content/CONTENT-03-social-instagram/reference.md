# Instagram Content — Reference: Reel Scripts & Visual Examples

Loaded on demand for detailed reel scripts, carousel structures, and Instagram content
patterns. The examples below are **skeletons**: topics, claims, account voices, and end
cards are decided per organization (see [`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md)). Bracketed `[...]` items
are filled from your brand decisions; everything else is reusable structure.

## Reel Script Examples

### Reel 1: Contrarian Hot Take (Brand account — 30s)

**Topic**: [a key message or content pillar]
**Audio**: Trending audio with a beat drop at the punchline

```
[0-3s] HOOK (to camera, direct):
"[One contrarian claim that exposes a gap your audience feels]"

[3-10s] SETUP:
"[Name the symptom the audience recognizes — what they've
tried, bought, or assumed — then the moment it falls apart.]"

[10-25s] POINT:
"[The reframe. Move from the surface problem to the real one —
tie it to a key-message point.]"

[25-30s] CLOSE + END CARD:
"[One punchy takeaway line]"
[Brand logo + the exact brand tagline — 2s]
```

**Caption**: "[One-line restatement of the point.] 🎯 [2–3 brand + niche hashtags]"

**Design Brief**: DESIGN BRIEF: 30s vertical reel | Fast cuts between to-camera and text overlays | Subtitle styling: white text, brand-color shadow, brand font | Trending audio | End card: brand logo + tagline

### Reel 2: Podcast Clip (Founder account — 60s)

**Topic**: [an episode theme tied to a content pillar]
**Audio**: Original audio from the podcast

```
[0-5s] HOOK (from podcast, mid-conversation):
"[A vivid, specific anecdote opener — a number or quote that
makes the viewer want the rest of the story.]"

[5-40s] THE STORY:
[Podcast audio continues — the full anecdote that illustrates
the point.]

[40-55s] THE LESSON:
"[The generalizable lesson the anecdote proves — one idea.]"

[55-60s] END CARD:
"Full episode → link in bio"
[Brand logo + founder name/title (from your account roster)]
```

**Caption**: "[Reactive line about the clip] 🎙️ Full episode → link in bio [2–3 hashtags]"

### Reel 3: Behind-the-Scenes (Brand account — 15s)

**Topic**: A day at the company
**Audio**: Trending lo-fi or workplace audio

```
[0-3s] TEXT OVERLAY: "What a [day] at [brand] actually looks like"

[3-5s] Shot: [an everyday opening moment — coffee, laptop opening]

[5-8s] Shot: [the work on screen — relevant to your pillars]

[8-12s] Shot: [a team moment — call, whiteboard, pointing at the work]

[12-15s] TEXT OVERLAY: "No filters. No script. Just the work. 🛠️"
[Brand logo fade in]
```

**Caption**: "[One line on what the work really looks like — unpolished, real.] [2–3 hashtags]"

### Reel 4: Quick One-Liner (Founder account — 15s)

**Topic**: [a sharp, single-claim key message]
**Audio**: Trending sound with a dramatic pause

```
[0-3s] HOOK (to camera):
"[Set up a conditional — 'If your [X] can't [Y]...']"

[3-5s] DRAMATIC PAUSE (trending sound beat)

[5-10s] PUNCHLINE:
"[...the consequence — a memorable, quotable inversion.]"

[10-15s] END:
"Follow for more [uncomfortable truths about your topic]."
[Brand logo]
```

**Caption**: "[One-line restatement of the punchline.] [2–3 hashtags]"

## Carousel Structure Examples

### Carousel 1: "The [Maturity / Capability] Ladder" (Educational — 5 slides)

A self-assessment ladder: where does the reader sit, and what does the top look like?

| Slide | Content | Design |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **1 (Hook)** | "Where does your company sit on the [Ladder name]?" | Bold text, brand-color background, simple graphic |
| **2** | "Level 1: [lowest-maturity state] — [one-line symptom]" | Icon + description, light bg |
| **3** | "Level 2: [middle state] — [one-line symptom]" | Icon + description |
| **4** | "Level 3: [target state] — [one-line outcome]" | Icon + description, highlighted as the goal |
| **5 (CTA)** | "Most companies are stuck at Level 1–2. We help them reach Level 3. → [brand handle]" | Brand logo, tagline, brand-color bg |

**Caption**: "Be honest — which level are you at right now? Most start at Level 1 or 2. No judgment. That's where progress begins. Tag someone who needs to see this 👇 [2–3 hashtags]"

---

### Carousel 2: "Old Way vs New Way" (Contrarian — 4 slides)

Contrast the legacy approach with how the brand works.

| Slide | Content | Design |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **1 (Hook)** | "The old way of [your category] is dead." | Bold text, brand-color bg |
| **2** | OLD: "[3–4 friction points of the legacy approach]" | Muted/greyed out, strikethrough |
| **3** | NEW: "[3–4 differentiators — your brand's]" | Bold brand colors, checkmarks |
| **4 (CTA)** | "That's how we work at [brand]. [Lead differentiator]. → [brand handle]" | Logo, tagline, brand-color bg |

## Story Content Patterns

### Story Sequence: "Ask Me Anything" (Weekly)

```
Story 1: Photo of workspace + text "AMA time 🎙️"
Story 2: Question sticker — "Ask me anything about [your topics]"
Story 3-6: Answer selected questions (mix text + video responses)
Story 7: "Thanks for the questions! More next week. Follow → [brand handle]"
```

### Story Sequence: "This or That" (Engagement)

```
Story 1: Poll — "[A vs B framed around your domain — a real tradeoff your audience debates]"
Story 2: Results + "Here's what I think:" (10s video response)
Story 3: Poll — "[A second, sharper either/or in your domain]"
```

### Story Sequence: "Event Coverage" (Live)

```
Story 1: Arrival photo + location tag
Story 2: Key moment (speaker on stage, panel)
Story 3: 15s video — personal takeaway
Story 4: Selfie with someone + tag
Story 5: "3 things I'm taking from today" (text + brand-color bg)
```

## Platform-Specific Formatting (universal)

| Element | Instagram Rule |
|---------|---------------|
| **Aspect Ratio** | 1:1 for posts, 9:16 for reels and stories |
| **Resolution** | 1080×1080 (post), 1080×1920 (reel/story) |
| **Caption length** | 150–300 chars (first line visible before "more") |
| **Hashtag placement** | End of caption or first comment |
| **Link** | Only in bio or story link sticker (no links in captions) |
| **Tagging** | Tag people in photo + mention in caption |
| **Alt text** | Add descriptive alt text for accessibility |
