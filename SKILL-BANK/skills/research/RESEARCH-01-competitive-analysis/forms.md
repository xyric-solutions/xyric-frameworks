# Competitive Analysis — Forms & Checklists

Ready-to-use intake, output template, and checklists. The "our position" specifics are
decided per organization (see [`ADAPT.md`](ADAPT.md)); this file never hardcodes them. The
competitor side is filled from sourced research.

---

## Our-Position Intake (the questionnaire for adapting this skill to your organization)

Only needed to frame *our* side of the comparison — the competitor research needs none
of this. Ask as one numbered list and **WAIT** for answers; record them wherever your team
keeps positioning decisions. Ask only what's missing.

1. **What is our offering** — the product or service this analysis is for (one line)?
2. **Business model** — how we make money (subscription, usage-based, services, one-time)?
3. **Target market / ICP** — who we serve (segments or tiers)?
4. **Top differentiators** — 3-5 things that make us genuinely different?
5. **Key messages** — the 3-9 promises we lead with?
6. **Pricing posture** — how we frame price relative to the market (premium / value / freemium)? (No exact figures needed in the template.)

> Use these answers as the offering, business model, target segments, differentiators, key
> messages, and pricing posture referenced throughout the output below.

## Quality Checklist

Use before delivering a competitive analysis.

### Coverage
- [ ] Direct competitors covered (top set in full 5P depth)
- [ ] At least some indirect competitors and substitutes included
- [ ] Potential entrants noted on a watch list
- [ ] Hidden competitors actively searched for (job posts, conferences, VC moves)

### Rigor
- [ ] Every factual claim has a dated source
- [ ] Multiple sources cross-referenced — no single-source claims
- [ ] Inference labeled as inference, not stated as fact
- [ ] No invented pricing, features, or figures

### Our-position framing
- [ ] `US` column/section filled from our decided position (or clearly labeled PLACEHOLDER)
- [ ] Opportunities grounded in our differentiators, not generic claims
- [ ] Recommendations are prioritized and each carries a rationale

### Freshness
- [ ] Analysis dated
- [ ] Refresh cadence stated (quarterly for fast-moving markets)
- [ ] Written as a living document, not a one-off
